Design

Will Gueble

2017/06/10

Categories: Standoff Tags: Game Design

Early in the design process, I turned to the chess programming community for inspiration on how to structure my game’s logic. I quickly encountered a slew of different strategies, but the debate between piece-based vs. board-based representation caught my eye. In the former, data about the game state is stored as a collection of pieces; in the latter, the data is formatted as a 2D array of tiles. Both options provide a similar degree of expressiveness and flexibility, so the decision came down to contextual requirements in the end. I chose a piece-based style in order to more readily describe the functionality of a shootout - the projectile collision properties of a shootout are benefitted by a piece-first mentality. Under this formulation, the outcome of a shootout can be easily calculated using hit detection rays originating from each live piece.
